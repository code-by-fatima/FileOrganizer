**Facts:**  
The appellants accepted offers of appointment and submitted joining reports. Their appointment letters contained one-year contract terms. The Labour Court had previously ruled in the matter, but the Labour Appellate Tribunal partially allowed the appeal, setting aside that judgment.

**Issues:**  
1. Whether the appellants were “employed persons” under Section 2(n) of the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Industrial and Commercial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 2013.  
2. Whether reinstatement or compensation was appropriate given the one-year contract terms in the appointment letters.

**Principles Held (Ratio Decendi):**  
1. The appellants, having accepted offers of appointment and submitted joining reports, became “employed persons” under Section 2(n) of the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Industrial and Commercial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 2013.  
2. Given the one-year contract terms in the appointment letters, reinstatement was not appropriate; instead, compensation of one year’s salary, calculated on gross salaries admissible at the time of appointment, was awarded in lieu of reinstatement.

**Consequences:**  
The Labour Court’s judgment was set aside. The appellants were awarded compensation equivalent to one year’s gross salary as admissible at the time of appointment, in lieu of reinstatement.
