**Decision:** Partially allowed; Labour Court’s judgment set aside.  
**Petitioner:** Appellants (employees who accepted offers of appointment and submitted joining reports)  
**Respondents:** Not specified in the partial summaries.  

**Facts:**  
The appellants accepted offers of appointment and submitted joining reports, thereby becoming employed persons under Section 2(n) of the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Industrial and Commercial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 2013. Their appointments were on a one-year contract basis. The Labour Court had previously ruled in the matter, but the Labour Appellate Tribunal partially allowed the appeal, setting aside that judgment.

**Issues:**  
1. Whether the appellants, having accepted offers of appointment and submitted joining reports, qualify as “employed persons” under Section 2(n) of the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Industrial and Commercial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 2013.  
2. What remedy is appropriate given that the appointments were on a one-year contract, and reinstatement may not be feasible.

**Principles Held (Ratio Decendi):**  
1. Acceptance of an offer of appointment and submission of a joining report renders a person an “employed person” under Section 2(n) of the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Industrial and Commercial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 2013.  
2. Where appointments are on a fixed-term contract (one year), reinstatement is not the appropriate remedy; instead, compensation equivalent to one year’s salary, calculated on the basis of gross salaries admissible at the time of appointment, is awarded in lieu of reinstatement.

**Consequences:**  
The Labour Court’s judgment was set aside. The appellants were awarded compensation of one year’s salary, calculated on the basis of gross salaries admissible at the time of appointment, in lieu of reinstatement.
