**Facts:** The petitioner was engaged as a temporary daily-wage Serviceman/Gardener. His services were terminated. He challenged the termination through a writ petition, seeking reinstatement and/or regularization.

**Issues:**
1. Whether the petitioner, as a daily-wage employee, had a vested right to reinstatement or regularization.
2. Whether the termination of the petitioner's services was lawful.
3. Whether the regularization of other employees conferred an enforceable right upon the petitioner.
4. Whether the impugned appellate order suffered from any procedural or jurisdictional defect.

**Principles Held (Ratio Decendi):**
1. A daily-wage employee engaged on a temporary basis possesses no vested right to reinstatement or regularization.
2. Termination of a daily-wage employee is lawful where the engagement is temporary, there is no statutory or policy basis for regularization, and a recruitment ban exists.
3. Regularization of other employees does not create an enforceable right for similarly situated daily-wage employees.
4. An appellate order that is procedurally sound and free from jurisdictional defect is not liable to be interfered with in constitutional jurisdiction.

**Consequences:** The writ petition was dismissed. The termination of the petitioner's services was upheld. The petitioner was not entitled to reinstatement or regularization.
